CST 328 – Digital Art & Design

Overview

📘 Course Overview

CST 328 explores the intersection of visual design, user interface (UI), user experience (UX), and digital media technologies. The course focuses on applying design principles and aesthetics to modern digital products, with an emphasis on usability, accessibility, and design thinking.

A special highlight of this course was the guidance of Professor Amir Attia, whose emphasis on strong design foundations, usability, and thoughtful critique helped shape both my design process and final outcomes.

Outcomes

🎯 Course Outcomes

  • Apply art and design fundamentals to digital and UI design work.
  • Analyze and critique designs using professional design terminology.
  • Practice user-centered design and design thinking methodologies.
  • Create high-fidelity UI designs using raster, vector, and prototyping tools.
  • Design accessible and usable interfaces following universal design principles.
  • Present and document design work professionally.
Highlights

🧩 Module Highlights

  • Foundations: Art vs. design, digital art forms, and visual principles.
  • Tools: Photoshop (raster), Illustrator (vector), and Figma (prototyping).
  • Game UI: Evaluating and redesigning game interfaces for usability.
  • UI Systems: Typography, layout grids, color theory, and interaction design.
  • Iteration: Critique-driven refinement and interactive prototyping.
Project

🎨 Final Project – Nova (UI Case Study)

The final project involved designing Nova, a student-centered productivity and collaboration application aimed at reducing academic fragmentation by unifying organization, peer collaboration, and opportunity discovery into a single platform.

The project followed a full user-centered design process, including research, ideation, wireframing, high-fidelity UI design, and iterative refinement.
